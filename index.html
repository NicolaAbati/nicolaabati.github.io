First things first

Hi, my name is Nicola.

I’m a web developer from Rimini, Italy.

I discovered my passion for coding while studying electronics and telecommunications in high school.

After graduating in psychology at the University of Bologna, I started my career in 2022 as a front end developer, focusing on mastering the fundamentals (HTML, CSS, and JavaScript) before moving on to modern frameworks. My first professional experience allowed me to deepen my knowledge of both front end and server-side development, working across different areas such as development, deployment, and application security. I see every challenge at work as an opportunity to learn and grow.

In my spare time I still enjoy building websites using the core tools I love, which allow me to create custom and tailored designs.

Beyond development, my other passion is music. I’ve played across my country and worked as a sound engineer, contributing to several musical projects. When I’m not at my desk, you’ll probably find me in a rehearsal studio or on stage.

Front End

When working on front end solutions, I focus on performance and simplicity.

I enjoy working with vanilla technologies, as they allow me to fully understand and control every aspect of what I create, keeping websites and applications lightweight and efficient.

However, I am comfortable using modern frameworks when a project requires them, and I can quickly adapt to different stacks and development environments.

I work with a variety of rendering strategies depending on project needs, including client-side rendering (CSR), server-side rendering (SSR), incremental static regeneration (ISR), and fully static sites. Choosing the right approach allows me to optimize performance, scalability, and user experience for each application.

My goal is to deliver simple, intuitive and maintainable interfaces, making the most out of each tool without unnecessary complexity.

Development, Delivery & Quality

I have experience working with both managed cloud infrastructures and self-hosted environments, which allows me to evaluate and choose the most suitable deployment strategy for each project. I regularly use Azure for deployment and DevOps workflows, while also being comfortable managing Linux-based VPS architectures when greater control or customization is required.

I have been involved in both static and dynamic code security analysis, using tools such as SonarQube and OWASP ZAP to monitor code quality and identify potential vulnerabilities. This has strengthened my attention to building applications that are reliable, maintainable, and secure by design.

I also work with content management systems such as WordPress and Directus, both in traditional and headless setups, integrating them into modern application architectures when content flexibility and scalability are required.
