First things first

Hi, my name is Nicola.

I’m a software developer from Rimini, Italy.

I first discovered my love for coding during my studies in electronics and telecommunications at school.

After graduating in psychology at University of Bologna I started out as a frontend developer, focusing on mastering the basics (HTML, CSS, and JS) before eventually moving on to modern frameworks. Now, at Lasersoft, I’ve had the chance to deepen my knowledge of both front-end and server-side development, tackling several aspects of my job (development, deployment, application security and more). I welcome every little challenge that my job presents me daily as a learning opportunity.

In my spare time I still love building websites, sticking to the fundamental tools that I love and allow me to craft custom and tailored designs.

Beyond development, my other passion is music. I’ve performed across much of Italy and worked as a sound engineer, contributing to the production of several musical projects. When I’m not at my desk, you’ll likely find me in a rehearsal studio or on stage.

Front End

When working on front-end solutions, I focus on performance and simplicity.

I enjoy working with vanilla technologies, as they allow me to fully understand and control every aspect of what I create, keeping applications lightweight and efficient.

However, I am comfortable using modern frameworks when a project requires them, and I can quickly adapt to different stacks and development environments.

My goal is to deliver simple, intuitive and maintainable interfaces, making the most out of each tool without unnecessary complexity.

I believe that well-designed front-end development is about finding the right balance between simplicity, flexibility, and scalability, while ensuring an excellent user experience.

Back end

I have worked on back-end systems ranging from small services to full-scale management applications, which has taught me that the key to delivering solid and maintainable solutions is selecting the right tools and architectural patterns for each situation, rather than automatically following the latest trends.

Over the years, this approach has allowed me to build systems that are not only reliable and efficient but also clear and easy for other developers to understand and extend.

I focus on practical, well-structured solutions that balance functionality, maintainability, and scalability, ensuring a smooth experience for both users and the teams that maintain the code over time.
